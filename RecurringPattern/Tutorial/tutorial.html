What is Recurring Pattern?

A Recurring Pattern (RP) is an ensemble of similar yet not necessarily identical "instances" in an image.

The term of "instances" refers to a visual area which distinctive features that could be undoubtedly grouped or segmented together. Each RP consists of two or more similar instances.

  • The four similar sun-shape instances could be grouped as a Recurring Pattern, even with scaling, rotation, shearing and color variations.

  • Correspondingly, the two similar thunder-shape instances could be grouped as another Recurring Pattern.

  • Finally, the ONE moon-shape instances CANNOT be grouped as a Recurring Pattern, because each RP shall consist of two or more similar instances.
